# My LeetCode Journey: From 0 to 500 Problems

When I started LeetCode, I couldn't solve an Easy problem without looking at the solution. Fast forward to today, and I've solved over 500 problems. Here's what I learned along the way.

## Why LeetCode?

Love it or hate it, **LeetCode-style interviews are the reality** for most tech companies. I decided to take it seriously because:

- Technical interviews are gatekeepers to great opportunities
- Problem-solving skills translate to real work
- It builds confidence in your coding abilities

## The Strategy That Worked

### 1. Start with Patterns, Not Problems

Instead of randomly solving problems, I focused on **patterns**:

- **Two Pointers** (arrays, strings)
- **Sliding Window** (subarray problems)
- **Fast & Slow Pointers** (linked lists, cycles)
- **Binary Search** (sorted arrays, search spaces)
- **DFS/BFS** (trees, graphs)
- **Dynamic Programming** (optimization problems)

Once you recognize the pattern, the solution becomes clearer.

### 2. The 70/30 Rule

I spent:
- **70% of time** on problems I could solve (with struggle)
- **30% of time** on problems just beyond my level

This kept me challenged without getting demoralized.

### 3. Consistency Over Intensity

I did **2-3 problems per day** instead of marathon sessions. Consistency builds muscle memory.

## My Progression

```
Month 1: Easy problems (2-3 hours each)
Month 2: Mix of Easy/Medium (1-2 hours each)
Month 3: Mostly Medium (30-60 min each)
Month 6: Medium in 20-30 min, Hard attempts
Month 12: 500+ problems, confident in interviews
```

## Favorite Problems

Some problems that really leveled up my skills:

1. **Two Sum** - The classic, teaches hash maps
2. **Longest Substring Without Repeating Characters** - Sliding window mastery
3. **LRU Cache** - Design + data structures
4. **Word Ladder** - BFS in disguise
5. **Coin Change** - DP fundamentals

## Interview Success

The grind paid off. In my last interview cycle:

- **5 companies interviewed**
- **4 offers received**
- **Solved all technical questions** (some with hints)

The confidence from LeetCode made a huge difference.

## Tips for Beginners

### Do:
✅ **Understand the solution**, don't just memorize  
✅ **Revisit problems** after a week  
✅ **Time yourself** to build speed  
✅ **Explain your thought process** out loud  
✅ **Focus on patterns**, not individual problems  

### Don't:
❌ **Give up after 5 minutes** - struggle for at least 20-30  
❌ **Skip Easy problems** - they build fundamentals  
❌ **Only do problems you can solve** - you won't grow  
❌ **Ignore time/space complexity** - it matters  

## The Reality Check

LeetCode isn't everything. It won't make you a better software engineer directly, but it will:

- **Open doors** to interviews
- **Build problem-solving skills**
- **Increase coding speed**
- **Boost confidence**

Real engineering is about building systems, working with teams, and shipping products. But LeetCode is the **price of admission** to many great companies.

## My Current Routine

Even after landing a job, I still do:
- **1-2 problems per week** to stay sharp
- **Focus on Hard problems** for the challenge
- **Participate in contests** occasionally

It's like going to the gym—maintenance mode keeps you ready.

---

**Bottom line:** LeetCode is a grind, but it's a worthwhile one. Start small, stay consistent, and focus on understanding patterns. You'll be surprised how quickly you improve.
